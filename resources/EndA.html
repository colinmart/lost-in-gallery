The stairs never end.

You run until your legs give out, buckling over and falling hard onto cold stone.

Wiping blood from your eyes, a gentle ringing in your head slowly fading away, you realize you can't hear anybody coming after you.
And that if you look hard enough, you can see the bottom of the spiral staircase.

There's nothing there but a doorway. Hurrying to the final step, you take a deep breath and peek around the corner.

It's another gallery.

Its somber blue-lacquered rooms stretch out without revealing any destination, artificial roses sprouting from cracks in the walls.
It's decorated with paintings and sculptures you've never seen before, host to strange back-rooms and twisting corridors
that defy conventional physics.
You can see something moving, deep within.

But with only a moment's hesitation, you find yourself crossing the threshold, a sheer determination driving your body forwards.

There's no way out but through.
